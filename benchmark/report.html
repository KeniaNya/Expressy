Method & honesty

How this was measured — and what not to over-read

Identical work. All five servers return byte-for-byte identical bodies and content-types on every route (checked by smoke.mjs before each run). Nobody wins by doing less.
Median of 3 × 6s after a 2s warmup, 64 connections, pipelining 1. Typical run-to-run spread was ~3%. The one blemish: Express·Node dropped 35 of 24,632 requests (0.14%) at 256 connections — it alone showed socket resets under saturation.
Same-host load. autocannon runs on the same machine as the server, so both share the CPU. Absolute rps would be higher on dedicated hardware — but the ratios between servers, which is the whole point, hold.
Express as shipped. Default config, including its weak-ETag hashing and X-Powered-By. A probe with both disabled (EXPRESS_TUNED=1) moved Express·Node by only ±8% — its cost here is the router and req/res layer, not optional features.
Expressy is Bun-only. It's built on Bun.serve and fetch Request/Response, so it can't run on Node. That's exactly why Express is measured on both runtimes — to separate the two effects rather than quietly credit the runtime to the framework.
